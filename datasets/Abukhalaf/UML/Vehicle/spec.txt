The domain centers on managing vehicle ownership, integrating individuals and their fleets under a structured system of rules and constraints to ensure compliance and clarity. A person, characterized by attributes such as name and age, serves as the central actor, capable of owning multiple vehicles, with a strict limitation of no more than three in total. Vehicles, encompassing cars and bikes, are distinguished by their color and inherit common features from the Vehicle class. Ownership rules stipulate that a vehicle owner must be at least 18 years old, ensuring maturity in vehicle management, while individuals under 18 are explicitly restricted from owning cars. Additionally, all cars in a person’s fleet must be black, with the number of black vehicles in any fleet capped at three, reinforcing uniformity and control. The domain ensures that at least one red car exists in the system, maintaining variety. Behavioral constraints for the Person class, such as incrementing age through birthday calls or setting age with non-negative values via setAge, promote data consistency. These interconnected elements and constraints define a robust, purpose-driven structure that emphasizes lawful ownership and operational reliability within the vehicle management ecosystem.
