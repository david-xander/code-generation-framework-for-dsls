Our air travel domain is centered around the structured management of airports, flights, airlines, and passengers, emphasizing controlled and consistent operations. Each flight connects two airports: one as the origin and the other as the destination. All departing flights originate exclusively from Amsterdam, which serves as our operational hub. Flights are operated by KLM, our designated airline, ensuring standardized service. Each flight has a calculated duration that matches the difference between its departure and arrival times, and the maximum number of passengers is capped at 1,000, maintaining safety and logistical efficiency.

Passengers are associated with flights based on their booking, with age requirements enforced to ensure compliance with individual flight policies. The system accommodates passengers requiring assistance, supporting inclusivity and accessibility. In some cases, passengers can also be affiliated with an airline in an executive capacity, such as a CEO, reflecting the broader organizational relationships within the system.

The domain model captures these relationships clearly, linking airports to their flights and flights to the airline and passengers. It ensures that origin and destination airports are always distinct, reinforcing operational logic. Together, this model ensures that air travel operates within a rigorous, predefined framework, delivering a streamlined and standardized experience.
