The QUDV (Quantities, Units, Dimensions, and Values) domain provides a robust framework for representing and managing units, quantities, and their interrelationships within scientific, engineering, and practical contexts. At its core, the domain is anchored by **Units** and **QuantityKinds**, where units serve as measurable representations of quantity kinds, such as meters for length or seconds for time. These units are organized within a **SystemOfUnits**, which aligns with a **SystemOfQuantities** to ensure coherence and consistency, particularly through constraints that mandate one base unit per base quantity within coherent systems. Specialized unit classes like **DerivedUnit** or **ConversionBasedUnit** encapsulate varying levels of complexity, such as products of base units or units derived via reference units and conversion factors. Prefixes, such as milli- or kilo-, extend units to enhance granularity and scale without introducing redundancy. Operational rules, like disallowing prefixed units as reference units or enforcing proportionality constraints in derived units, ensure logical consistency and adherence to scientific standards. By uniting these entities under a formalized structure, the domain facilitates precision, interoperability, and reliable transformation of units and quantities across diverse applications, embodying the principles of coherence and scalability fundamental to measurement systems.
