The domain represents a structured ecosystem where individuals, companies, banks, and marital unions interact to model real-world social and economic dynamics. At its core, a **Person** is characterized by demographic attributes such as name, gender, age, and marital status, with relationships connecting them as family members, spouses, employees, or customers. **Companies** employ people under constraints ensuring diverse age representation, including an employee over 50, and disallowing child labor. A company’s size is capped at 50 employees, its stock price remains positive, and its workforce includes unique individuals such as "Jack." **Marriages** formalize spousal relationships, with constraints limiting a person to one concurrent marriage and enforcing rules on maiden names for married women. **Jobs** link individuals to companies, contributing to their income through aggregating salaries. Meanwhile, **Banks** manage financial accounts, associating customers with specific accounts. This interconnected system is further enriched by familial ties, where **Persons** recursively derive descendants from their lineage. Operational limits ensure realism, such as the total population of individuals capped at 100 and employees’ age restricted to 65. This domain highlights the interplay of organizational and social structures, offering a robust framework to explore relationships, economic flows, and societal norms.
