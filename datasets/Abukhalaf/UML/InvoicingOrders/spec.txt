The domain represents a system for managing product inventories and customer orders, ensuring seamless alignment between supply and demand. At its core are two entities: **Product**, which tracks inventory with uniquely identified items and a non-negative stock level, and **Order**, which represents customer requests for specific product quantities. Each order is tied to a product through the OrderProduct relationship, reflecting the dependency of orders on the product's availability. The system enforces strict constraints to maintain integrity and operational accuracy. Products must have unique identifiers, and their stock levels can only be adjusted positively through the supply process or reduced by releasing quantities when fulfilling orders. Orders, uniquely identified as well, must specify a valid product and a positive quantity, and their state transitions are tightly controlled. An order begins as "pending" and can only be invoiced if the product’s stock can fulfill the requested quantity, subsequently reducing inventory. Conversely, invoiced orders can be canceled, returning the allocated stock. These constraints ensure consistency, prevent over-allocation, and maintain real-time accuracy in stock and order statuses, allowing stakeholders to confidently manage inventory levels and customer commitments within a structured, rule-driven environment.
