The Library Domain orchestrates the borrowing, reserving, and categorization of books, aligning member privileges with predefined rules to ensure equitable access. At its core, the system manages **Books**, which are grouped into **Categories** defining thematic or subject-based classifications, while **Book Copies** represent individual, borrowable instances. Members, classified under distinct **Member Categories**, interact with these entities under operational constraints designed to balance availability and demand. A member can borrow a **Book Copy**, which becomes associated with them through a **Loan** specifying its start and end dates, constrained by the member's category-specific borrowing limits. Additionally, books may be placed on hold by multiple members in an ordered queue, though no member may duplicate their hold request for the same title. The domain also governs interdependencies between **Book Categories** and **Member Categories**, ensuring every pairing has a maximum loan period defined by the **Loan Period** entity. Constraints such as prohibiting concurrent loans and reserves on the same copy, limiting holds per category, and enforcing borrowing caps for each member's category sustain fairness and resource availability. This structured yet adaptable framework enables efficient library operations, fostering a reliable and user-friendly borrowing experience while preserving system integrity.
