The Employment Agency domain encapsulates the structure and dynamics of managing workforce assignments, departmental operations, and project allocations within an organization. Central to the domain is the **Employee**, uniquely identified by their SSN and characterized by personal, employment, and supervisory attributes. Employees belong to **Departments**, where a single manager oversees operations while adhering to constraints such as being older and earning more than their subordinates. Departments, in turn, control **Projects**, ensuring alignment between project locations and departmental operations. Employees contribute to these projects within strict hour limits and work only on projects managed by their respective departments. Supervisory relationships emphasize hierarchy, prohibiting self-supervision and cyclic dependencies. **Dependents**, linked to employees by unique identifiers, further personalize the workforce structure. Rigorous constraints ensure compliance, such as maintaining an employee’s age above 18, enforcing sufficient work hours for non-supervisors, and limiting the number of employees contributing minimal effort to projects. By maintaining these rules, the domain achieves operational harmony, supporting scalable workforce management, project execution, and accountability across organizational layers, all while aligning with real-world legal and logistical requirements.
