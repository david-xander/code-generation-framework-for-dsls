The Tournament domain models a structured environment for competitive events, emphasizing the organization and management of players, matches, and tournaments. A **Tournament** serves as the central entity, characterized by its name and schedule, and is responsible for orchestrating matches and managing player participation. Matches are pivotal events within a tournament, each bound by start and end dates, and facilitated by player interactions that determine scores. Players, the core participants, interact dynamically with tournaments and matches, highlighting their critical role in driving competition. Operational integrity is enforced through constraints: matches must occur within the tournament’s timeframe, ensuring temporal alignment, while a mandatory match on the first day underscores the importance of timely engagement. Furthermore, the structure ensures exclusivity, with players limited to one tournament or match at a time, maintaining clear boundaries and avoiding conflicts. Every tournament requires sufficient scale, with at least 33 matches, each comprising at least 10 players, to ensure competitive depth and engagement. These operational guidelines reinforce the domain’s purpose: to provide a fair, well-organized framework for competitive play, promoting integrity and structured gameplay while supporting scalability and participant engagement. The relationships and rules harmonize to deliver a robust and coherent system.
