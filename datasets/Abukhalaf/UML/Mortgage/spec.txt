The domain represents a mortgage management system designed to facilitate and regulate the lending process for property ownership. At its core are individuals, represented as persons, who can own one or more houses and seek financing through mortgages. A person’s eligibility to obtain a mortgage hinges on their income and ownership of the property being financed. Specifically, a house can only serve as security for a mortgage if it is owned by the borrower, ensuring alignment between asset ownership and financial liability. Mortgages are characterized by a principal amount, monthly payments, and a fixed term defined by start and end dates, with constraints ensuring the start date precedes the end date. The financial integrity of the system is upheld through additional rules, such as limiting a borrower’s total mortgage obligations to no more than 30% of their salary and ensuring that the total secured debt on a house does not exceed its value. Unique identifiers, like the social security number for each person, reinforce data integrity. Together, these relationships and rules establish a structured yet flexible framework for managing property financing, balancing stakeholder interests, and safeguarding financial soundness within the system.
